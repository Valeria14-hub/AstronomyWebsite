Stars

Facts:

  • Stars are made up of glowing, ionized gas which the majority is hydrogen and helium.
  • A star can shine up to tens of billions of years.
  • You can see the 3 biggest stars - Sirius, Canopus, and Alpha Centauri - in the southern night sky (The southern hemisphere, to be more specific, in New Zealand).
  • In New Zealand, you can see the centre of the Milky Way.
  • The star's color depends on the temperature e.g. blue stars are generally very hot while red/orange stars are a bit cooler.
  • Most Blue stars are generally bigger than the red ones, otherwise known as drawf stars for some stars.
  • Stars are born in molecular clouds, otherwise known as a nebula.
  • The sun is also a star.
  • The largest star type (even bigger than the sun) is the red supergiant.
  • Stars have a longer life span than humans, including the shortest-lived stars too.
